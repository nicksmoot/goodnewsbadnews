GoodNewsBadNews.com Product + Website Brief

1. Core Concept

Good News Bad News is a local civic signal platform.

It helps a community see itself clearly by collecting, organizing, verifying, and publishing what residents are seeing on the ground.

It is not a normal newspaper.
It is not a neighborhood complaint board.
It is not Nextdoor.
It is not a city government portal.
It is not anonymous gossip.

It is a structured civic intelligence layer where residents can share:

* Good things happening in the community
* Bad things that need attention
* Mixed stories that show both progress and problems
* Early signals before they become public crises
* Ideas, resources, and opportunities
* Local wins that deserve amplification
* Patterns that institutions may be missing

The simplest framing:

Every city has good news and bad news. Both matter. Good News Bad News gives residents a trusted place to surface what is working, what is breaking, and what needs attention next.

For the Spokane launch, the positioning can be:

Spokane has two stories. Both matter.

2. Product Purpose

The platform exists to solve a civic information problem.

Most communities are flying blind. Local newspapers are shrinking. Social media is noisy. City governments often hear from only the loudest residents. Institutions miss weak signals until they become crises. Positive work often goes unseen. Problems are either ignored or distorted.

Good News Bad News creates a moderated, community-powered signal system.

It should help answer:

* What is actually happening in this city?
* What are residents seeing before official systems notice?
* Where are people making progress?
* Where are systems failing?
* What should leaders, residents, funders, nonprofits, churches, schools, and businesses pay attention to?
* What deserves celebration?
* What needs action?

The site should feel like a cross between:

* A local civic newsroom
* A moderated community reporting tool
* A signal dashboard
* A public square with rules
* A neighborhood early-warning system
* A storytelling engine for city improvement

Brand Promise

A clearer signal for your city.

Good News Bad News helps communities separate real local signal from noise.

Core Message

We collect the good, the bad, and the complicated stories shaping your city, then organize them into a trusted local signal feed.

Tone

The tone should be:

* Clear
* Civic
* Trustworthy
* Local
* Direct
* Human
* Moderated
* Constructive
* Slightly bold
* Not partisan
* Not sensational
* Not corporate
* Not bureaucratic

This cannot feel like a government website.
It also cannot feel like a rant board.

It should feel like a serious local civic product with cultural edge.

Brand Tension

The brand works because of the tension in the name.

Good News = hope, progress, wins, solutions, people doing good work.
Bad News = problems, failures, warning signs, things that need fixing.

But the real power is in Both.

Many stories are not purely good or bad. The site should make room for nuance.

Example:

A new shelter opened downtown. Good news: more beds. Bad news: neighbors say communication has been poor. Both can be true.

8. Content Categories

The product should be organized around simple visible categories.

Primary Editorial Buckets

Good News

Positive local stories, wins, improvements, inspiring people, promising programs.

Examples:

* A local teacher created an after-school robotics club
* A church opened a warming center
* A business hired 20 young people
* A park cleanup brought out 80 residents
* A neighborhood reduced vandalism through local coordination

Bad News

Problems, patterns, failures, risks, gaps, and early warnings.

Examples:

* A bus stop has become unsafe after dark
* Parents report recurring bullying near a school
* A public restroom has been closed for months
* A dangerous intersection has repeated crashes
* A nonprofit program is overwhelmed

Both

Nuanced stories where progress and problems exist together.

Examples:

* New housing is being built, but neighbors worry about infrastructure
* Downtown foot traffic is up, but small businesses say security costs are rising
* A new city program is helping people, but access is confusing
* A school added mental health resources, but waitlists remain long

Signals

Shorter observations that may not yet be full stories.

Examples:

* “Three businesses on this block now have broken windows.”
* “The new trail connection is getting heavy use.”
* “Several parents mentioned kids skipping lunch because of cost/confusion.”
* “People are gathering at this corner every morning looking for day work.”

Opportunities

Ways to help, join, fund, volunteer, attend, or respond.

Examples:

* Volunteer cleanup
* Public meeting
* Donation drive
* Youth mentoring
* Local hiring fair
* Neighborhood watch training
* Community health event

9. Submission Flow

The submission flow is the most important product experience.

It should be fast, structured, and clear.

CTA Language

Use:

Submit a Signal

Not just:

Submit News

Because residents may not know whether what they saw is “news.” They do know they saw something worth reporting.

Submission Form Fields

Required Fields

1. What kind of signal is this?

Options:

* Good News
* Bad News
* Both
* Opportunity
* Question
* Pattern / Trend
* Resource
* Event
* Unsure

2. What happened or what are you seeing?

Long text field.

Prompt:

Describe what you saw, heard, experienced, or believe the community should know. Be specific. Avoid personal attacks or unsupported claims.

3. Where is this happening?

Fields:

* Neighborhood
* Address or cross-streets, optional
* City
* Zip code
* Map pin, optional

4. Topic

Checkboxes:

* Housing
* Homelessness
* Public safety
* Schools
* Youth
* Health
* Business
* Jobs
* Downtown
* Transportation
* Parks
* Faith/community
* Government
* Addiction/recovery
* Mental health
* Events
* Arts/culture
* Environment
* Other

5. Is there evidence or context?

Options:

* I saw this myself
* Someone told me directly
* I have photos/video
* I have documents
* I have a link
* I am asking the team to look into it
* This is a pattern I have noticed

6. Upload photos or files

Optional.

Important copy:

Only upload photos you have the right to share. Do not upload private information, medical information, license plates, minors’ faces without permission, or anything intended to shame a person.

7. Can we contact you?

Options:

* Yes, I can be contacted
* Yes, but do not publish my name
* No, submit anonymously

Fields:

* Name
* Email
* Phone, optional

8. Publication preference

Options:

* You may publish my name
* Keep me anonymous if published
* Contact me before publishing
* Do not publish directly, use as background signal only

9. Agreement checkbox

Required:

I understand this platform does not allow doxxing, personal attacks, threats, private personal information, or unsupported accusations against private individuals.

Form Completion Message

After submit:

Signal received. Our team will review it before anything is published. Some submissions become public stories, some become background signals, and some may be combined with other reports to identify patterns.

10. Moderation Rules

This is essential.

The site needs public standards and internal moderation tools.

Public Moderation Principles

Create a page called:

Community Standards

Core language:

Good News Bad News is built for civic signal, not gossip. We publish stories, patterns, concerns, and opportunities that help the community understand itself and respond. We do not publish doxxing, threats, personal attacks, unsupported accusations, private information, or content designed to shame individuals.

Not Allowed

* Doxxing
* Private addresses of individuals
* Phone numbers or private emails
* Accusations against private individuals without verification
* Medical/private personal information
* Threats
* Harassment
* Hate speech
* Photos intended to humiliate
* Naming minors in sensitive contexts
* Legal allegations without documentation
* “This person is a criminal” type claims
* Unverified rumors

Allowed

* Firsthand observations
* Public concerns about places, systems, policies, and institutions
* Documented facts
* Photos of public conditions
* Constructive complaints
* Community wins
* Resident experiences
* Public meeting notes
* Resource gaps
* Patterns noticed by multiple residents

Internal Review Statuses

Each submission should have admin statuses:

* New
* Needs Review
* Needs Follow-up
* Verified
* Publish as Signal
* Publish as Story
* Merge into Pattern
* Reject
* Archive
* Legal/Escalation Review

11. Content Types

The platform should support several content formats.

Signal Card

Short post format.

Fields:

* Title
* Category: Good / Bad / Both / Opportunity / Signal
* Neighborhood
* Topic tags
* Short summary
* Status: submitted, reviewed, verified, updated
* Date
* Optional photo
* CTA: “I’ve seen this too” / “I can help” / “Follow updates”

Example:

More broken streetlights reported near Monroe
Bad News · Public Safety · North Spokane
Multiple residents have reported dark blocks near Monroe and Montgomery. The issue has been submitted for review and may be updated as more information comes in.

Story Article

Longer editorial format.

Fields:

* Headline
* Deck/subheadline
* Category
* Author/contributor
* Published date
* Neighborhood
* Topic tags
* Body
* Related signals
* What happens next
* How to help / who to contact

Pattern Report

Used when many small signals point to a larger issue.

Example:

Pattern: Parents report rising safety concerns near three downtown bus stops

Sections:

* What residents are reporting
* Where it is happening
* Why it matters
* What is confirmed
* What is still unclear
* What leaders or organizations are doing
* How residents can contribute more information

Weekly Digest

Email and web post.

Sections:

* 3 Good News items
* 3 Bad News items
* 1 Both story
* 3 Signals to watch
* 3 Ways to help
* 1 Question for the community

23. Verification Model

The site needs a clear verification system.

Suggested Labels

Submitted

Received but not reviewed.

Under Review

Team is reviewing.

Reviewed

Reviewed for standards and basic plausibility.

Verified

Confirmed with evidence, multiple sources, official data, or direct documentation.

Updated

New information added.

Resolved

Problem appears addressed or story has reached a clear conclusion.

Background Signal

Not published, but included in internal pattern tracking.

24. Legal / Risk Design

The platform should be designed to reduce legal exposure.

Key Product Rules

* Avoid publishing accusations against private individuals
* Focus on systems, places, conditions, and patterns
* Use neutral language
* Keep records of source material
* Allow corrections
* Do not publish sensitive private information
* Do not publish medical details unless safely anonymized
* Do not identify minors in sensitive contexts
* Do not present unverified claims as confirmed facts

Safer Language Pattern

Instead of:

John Smith is stealing from the nonprofit.

Use:

A resident submitted a concern about financial transparency at a local nonprofit. The platform has not independently verified the claim and will not publish personal accusations without documentation.

Instead of:

This homeless camp is full of criminals.

Use:

Residents near the area reported safety and sanitation concerns. The submission is under review and may be combined with other reports into a broader pattern report.
